Electricity Cost Reduction Strategies for Smart Homes

Understanding your electricity bill and the levers available to reduce it is the foundation of effective home energy management.

Understanding Time-of-Use (TOU) Tariffs:
- TOU tariffs charge different rates depending on the time of day and sometimes the season.
- Typical structure: Off-peak (~$0.09/kWh), Mid-peak (~$0.13/kWh), Peak (~$0.21/kWh).
- Shifting just 30% of daily consumption from peak to off-peak hours can reduce electricity bills by 10–18%.

Demand Charge Avoidance:
- Some commercial and residential tariffs include a demand charge based on your peak 15-minute consumption during the billing period.
- A single high-draw event (e.g., EV + AC + oven simultaneously) can increase your demand charge for the entire month.
- Stagger large appliance start times by 15–30 minutes to avoid simultaneous peaks.

High-Impact Appliances to Shift:
- Electric water heater: Run a timer to heat water during off-peak hours and rely on thermal storage during peak periods.
- HVAC: Pre-cool or pre-heat your home before peak hours begin. A 2-degree buffer can hold comfort for 1–2 hours without HVAC running.
- Dishwasher and washing machine: Use delay-start features to run at midnight or during solar peak hours.
- Pool pump: Run for 6–8 hours during off-peak or solar hours instead of peak hours.

Phantom Load Elimination:
- Electronics in standby mode (TVs, game consoles, chargers) consume 5–10% of total household electricity.
- Use smart power strips that cut power to peripherals when the main device is off.
- Unplug chargers and small appliances when not in use.

ROI Calculations:
- LED upgrade: Replacing 20 incandescent bulbs (60W) with LEDs (9W) saves ~1 kWh/day. At $0.13/kWh: $47/year. Payback: 3–6 months.
- Smart thermostat: Average savings of $140–$180/year. Payback: 1–2 years.
- Solar panels (6 kW system): Generates ~8,000 kWh/year. Saves $960/year at $0.12/kWh. Payback: 7–10 years with incentives.
- Battery storage (10 kWh): Avoids ~1,200 kWh of peak consumption/year. Saves $144/year at $0.12 peak-to-off-peak differential. Payback: 14–18 years standalone.

Energy Audit Benefits:
- A professional energy audit identifies the highest-impact improvements for your specific home.
- Common findings: poor insulation, air leaks around windows/doors, outdated HVAC equipment.
- DIY audit: walk through the home with an infrared thermometer and check all appliance energy labels.

Behavioural Changes with High Impact:
- Reduce water heater temperature from 60°C to 49°C: saves 6–10% of water heating costs.
- Wash clothes in cold water: reduces washing machine energy use by 75–90%.
- Use natural ventilation in evenings instead of AC when outdoor temperature drops below indoor temperature.
- Cook multiple meals at once to reduce oven usage frequency.
