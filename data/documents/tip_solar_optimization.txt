Solar Self-Consumption and Optimization Strategies

Solar panels generate the most electricity between 9 AM and 3 PM, with peak output typically around solar noon (12:00–13:00). Aligning high-consumption activities with this window dramatically reduces grid dependency and lowers electricity bills.

Self-Consumption Strategies:
- Run dishwashers, washing machines, and pool pumps between 10 AM and 2 PM when solar output is highest.
- Charge electric vehicles during midday hours when you have excess solar generation, rather than overnight from the grid.
- Use a smart energy monitor to track real-time solar output and automatically shift loads.

Battery Storage:
- Home batteries (e.g., Tesla Powerwall, Enphase IQ Battery) store excess solar energy generated during the day for use in peak evening hours (18:00–22:00), avoiding the highest electricity rates.
- Ideal battery capacity is roughly 70–80% of your average daily solar generation.
- Battery ROI typically ranges from 8–12 years depending on local electricity prices and incentive programs.

Net Metering:
- Under net metering, excess solar energy exported to the grid earns credits on your bill at a set rate (the feed-in tariff).
- Net metering policies vary by location. Some utilities offer 1:1 credits; others pay a reduced export rate.
- Maximising self-consumption is generally more valuable than exporting, especially where feed-in tariffs are low.

Panel Orientation and Tilt:
- In the northern hemisphere, south-facing panels at a tilt equal to your latitude capture the most annual energy.
- East-west split installations capture more morning and afternoon light, increasing total self-consumption windows.
- Shade from trees or buildings during any part of the day can reduce system output by 20–50%.

Monitoring and Maintenance:
- Clean panels quarterly to remove dust and debris, which can reduce output by 5–15%.
- Monitor inverter performance monthly. A sudden drop in output often indicates a faulty panel or connection.
- Most modern systems provide real-time generation data via mobile apps.

Seasonal Variation:
- Solar generation is typically 2–3 times higher in summer than winter.
- Adjust appliance scheduling seasonally: maximise daytime use in summer; rely more on off-peak grid hours in winter.
